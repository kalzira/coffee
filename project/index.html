Coffee effects on organism

Health research on coffee swings back and forth between good and bad news more frequently than almost any other topic. When you hear about one study claiming health benefits while another harps on a list of negatives, it’s easy to get jaded and stop paying attention. To bring some focus, here’s a summary of both good and bad effects of coffee from a selection of coffee studies.

Positive

  • Raise your RMR - your resting metabolic rate is basically the rate at which you body burns fat while doing nothing. Caffeine can raise your RMR by 3–11% depending on the dosage. (American Journal of Clinical Nutrition)
  • Coffee has been shown to delay the onset of Alzheimer’s According the the European Journal of Neurology
  • Coffee boosts brain function and even aids in long-term memory A study done at the University of California showed that adults who took 200mg of caffeine before their memory test had much better scores than those who did not.
  • Coffee can protect against Type II Diabetes which is a precursor to Alzheimer’s According to a study out of the National Center for Biotechnology Information six cups a day of coffee was associated with a 33% lower risk of type 2 diabetes.
  • Coffee helps prevent age related muscle loss Coventry University sports scientists found this in an animal study

Negative

  • Cortisol - Too much coffee can create an environment of excess acidity, and this triggers the hormone cortisol. Cortisol stresses you out and impairs your ability to lose fat.
  • Diuretic - Caffeine makes you piss. This can cause dehydration and electrolyte loss. While this is not a BAD thing - it can lead to complications if you dont properly hydrate habitually.
  • Caffeine Tolerance - Everyone knows that when you are taking drugs you need more and more to get the same effect as the last dose. Make no mistake Caffeine is a drug and acts like all the others in this respect.

Conclusion

Many of these negative effects can be negated by simply:
  • Not drinking too much - keep it under 500mgs of caffeine per day. A cup of Arabica coffee( the good stuff) contains about 120mgs on average. Robusta coffee (think instant coffee) contains about 180mgs per cup on average.
  • Cycling your coffee - abstain from coffee for one month out of every four
  • Keep hydrated - keep an eye on the color of your pee
  • Keep in mind that this is only with black coffee! Adding tons of sugar and crazy stuff can do all sorts of terrible things to your body.
